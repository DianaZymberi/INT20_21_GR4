How to read Tarot Cards

After I have jazzed up the deck, I proceed with the type of intuitive reading that I offer my clients. As I am a go-big or go-home type of girl, I don’t use just one deck. On any given day I have ten to twenty decks of different cards mixed in the spread I use in my work. The cards you will find in my stack range from mantra cards and spirit animal cards, to goddess cards and traditional Rider-Waite style decks. I like to keep things interesting and it’s fun to have a dynamic palette of messages and images.

Once a client has chosen the number of cards that feels right, we lay out their personal visual narrative and begin the reading. In a session with me you can pick as many cards as you want—however, what you pick you keep. Why do I do this? One, it helps my clients flex their intuitive muscle by picking what they need to hear about that day. One hundred times out of one hundred, a client picks a card and looks at it, and it immediately resonates with a subject matter we discussed prior to picking a card.

Following the reading, I send my clients home with Shamanic Mystical Homework: I ask that a client takes all the cards they received in a session and put them somewhere that is personal and within their everyday sphere, like an altar, bathroom mirror, or journal. I ask them to look at the cards every once in awhile and have a gut check on how the process is going.

Once you feel like you have worked through the energy of the cards, it’s time to part ways. How? I ask that you say goodbye to your personal story around the card and thank it for all the teachings, lessons, and blessings it has shown you. In shamanism, you are always working with the elements to help heal and shift energy. We will work with the elements of air, fire, or water in this exercise. I ask that you burn, bury, or set the card to sea. What does this do? We must look at ourselves like a house or container. If we want to bring new energy in, we must first make room by letting go of old energy. There is only so much room in our container, right? Since everything starts at an energetic level before it becomes mental, physical, or emotional, we are kick-starting the process by saying goodbye and thank you to those old stories, habits, or lessons that are no longer serving where we are now and where we are headed. It’s exactly like spring cleaning your closet. You don’t want to hold onto clothes that no longer fit you, represent your career path, or have holes in them. HOT TIP: A great time to burn, bury, or set cards to sea is at the full moon, when we release energy that no longer serves us.

(If you choose to do my method you will eventually need to buy more tarot cards or a completely new deck. That is your personal preference. I am constantly refilling my stack as I use them in sessions every day.)
